Tempura

Description

Tempura is a traditional Japanese dish that became popular during the Edo era (1603–1867). It's made by coating vegetables or seafood in a thin batter and deep-frying it. The word "tempura" comes from the Portuguese word tempero, which means "seasoning".

Ingredients

  • Shrimp
  • fish
  • Chicken
  • Eggs
  • Vegetables- lotus seeds, onions, mushrooms
  • Shiso leaves

Steps

  1. Clean the Shrimp under running water and remove the heads. Devein the shrimp. Straighten them on a plate over paper towels to dry.
  2. Prep the vegetables by washing and drying them before dunking them in batter

  3. Tempura Batter

    • In a heavy-bottomed pot, add about 2 inches of oil from the bottom of the pot. Heat the oil to 350 °F degrees.
    • In a mixing bowl, add the flour and half of potato starch and mix thoroughly.
    • In another bowl, add the egg and mix in the cold vodka. Then add the carbonated water.
  4. Carefully lower the battered ingredients into the oil. The vegetables cook fairly quickly so watch them carefully.
